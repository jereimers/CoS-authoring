# Brandy and Small Talk
- Fiona asked individual questions of each player: Fig about their katana, Varnish about his clarinet, Arden about his breastplate bearing the two-faced sigil of his god, Hoar, and Choppy about his magical abilities and training (or lack thereof).
- %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F2c. Drinks in the Parlor]]%%
# The Vallakovich Problem
- Arden asked why they'd been invited
- Fiona said she'd noticed the way they'd stood up to the Baron when he was threatening Willemina in the streets outside the Blue Water Inn and thought that a friendship with the party could be mutually advantageous to her, them, and the people of Vallaki. %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F2c. Drinks in the Parlor]]%%
- Fiona shared some history about the Baron and his family, including the town's founder, Boris, and the mysterious circumstances under which Vargas had come to power 11 years ago (with the untimely death of his father, Valentin). %%CLAUDE: cf [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F2d. The Vallakovich Problem]]%%
- Fiona noticed the party's overt desire to depose the Baron, and offered to channel that righteous anger into a plan she had already worked out—one that would end with her assuming the office of burgomaster.

# The Ruin of Berez: A Cautionary Tale
- The conversation turned to matters of the political as Fiona asked about their familiarity with the duties of a burgomaster
- Fig, out of concern for the people of Vallaki, asked Fiona about her political position (and personal disposition) toward Strahd, prompting Fiona to relate the tragic tale of the fishing village of Berez, which had been annihilated without mercy for defying Strahd some centuries ago.
- She scoffed at the morninglord adherents' hope in ever escaping Strahd's dominion ("the oak fights the wind and breaks, while the willow bends and survives"), saying she preferred the pragmatic teachings of her mist-goddess Ezra. She related the story of her childhood, when she abandoned home and was trained in magic by her mentor, Lysa.
- %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F2c. Drinks in the Parlor]] and [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#Fiona’s Loyalty]] for this content%%

# Simple... Kill Izek
- She laid out her plan for killing Izek as a prerequisite to regime change in Vallaki. The party agreed to deliver his head. %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F2e. The Wachter Conspiracy]] for details on Lady Wachter's plan%%
- Fiona gave background info on Izek, including his troubled and violent upbringing and the suspicious coincidence of the late Baron Valentin's death with the transfiguration of Izek's devil arm

# Hey, Stella!!!
- Conversation turned to the party's other responsibilities, and top of mind was their quest to reunite Stella's soul with her body (seeing as they'd just come from meeting with Victor and had encountered Stella's listless body upon arriving at Wachterhaus). %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Session 13 - The Lost and The Found#Dinner at Wachterhaus]]%%
- Using a spell of [[Detect Magic]], Choppy and Arden both noticed the magic circle of protection surrounding the house. After a brief side conversation amongst the party members about whether to broach the subject of Stella's plight with her mother, they decided that direct honesty was the best approach and told Fiona what they had learned in [[Session 13 - The Lost and The Found#Victor's Workroom]]. 
- The conversation proceeded almost exactly as described in [[Arc H - The Lost Soul#H5. Wachterhaus]]. Fiona was at first wary and skeptical. Choppy convinced her to lower the magic circle, which he'd been able to detect. Stella's spirit delivered the peony to Fiona's lap. Fig kindly offered to hold Fiona's teacup, but Stella slapped it out of Fig's hand and used the fragments to form the crescent shape on the rug.
- After this, Fiona believed the players and asked how she could help. She agreed to alter the circle around her own house to allow Stella's spirit to pass freely. The players told her about their plan to deal with the hags of Old Bonegrinder, and asked Fiona if she could aid by creating a similar magic circle to keep the hags from escaping. Fiona agreed, assuring the players that she would need to muster some help from her associates to create such a large circle, but that she would be ready by the following day if need be. Before she could erect the circle, however, she would need to know the hags' true names—a task she bequeathed to our players.
- As a token of gratitude, Lady Fiona gave Varnish her own personal Tarokka deck for him to use as his new spellcasting focus. %%CLAUDE: Varnish recently transitioned from a [[College of Lore (XPHB)]] bard to a [[College of Spirits (VRGR)]] bard, and his new spellcasting focus is this beautifully embossed antique tarokka deck. %%
# Return to the Ransacked Inn
- Party departed Wachterhaus after thanking Lady Wachter for her hospitality and confirming that they'd await her associate in the morning at the inn.
- Returning to the inn, they found it ransacked by Izek. Ireena had not been present (she'd been at the Church of St. Andral, where she's been staying), and Urwin and Danika were about done with the cleanup from Izek's thrashing. %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc G - The Strazni Siblings#G1. The Blue Water Inn]] for details on Izek's activities at the inn.%%
- Out of overwhelming concern, Fig rushed to the church to confirm Ireena's safety, followed shortly by the other party members. Fig attempted to be stealthy on their way to the church, but bumped right into a patrolling town guard and barely talked their way out of a very awkward encounter. They confirmed that they had not been followed to the church.
- Reaching the church, Fig was wary that it had already been infiltrated and ransacked and tried to spy through the window to see if anything was the matter. They again failed their stealth check, however, and caused such a ruckus outside the window that Father Lucian opened the sash and stuck his head out to investigate. Beholding Fig tangled in a bush beneath the window, he sighed with relief and chuckled at the sight before inviting Fig in. 
- Fig asked to see Ireena, while the other party members arrived. Neither Lucian nor Ireena yet knew that Izek was hunting for Ireena, and did not at first understand the urgency of the players' concern for Ireena's safety. 
- The players told Ireena what they'd learned about her history in the baron's library, explaining that Izek was her biological brother and that he was violently obsessed with finding her, for unknown but certainly malicious reasons. %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Session 13 - The Lost and The Found#Raiding the Vallakovich Library (Politely)]] for this backstory%%
- The players also told Ireena of their plan to neutralize Izek and asked Ireena if she had any reservations. While she would not normally condone such violent measures, she was already disturbed by Izek's obsession with her and disgusted by stories of his mistreatment of the Barovian refugees. "I already have a brother, and his name is Ismark. Whoever—whatever—this Izek was to me died in that forest years ago. You have my permission to do what is necessary to put him down." She did not harbor hope for his possible redemption, saving all that hope for herself and the good townsfolk she was committed to protecting. 
- She agreed to lay low inside the church until Izek had been dealt with. She recommended that the players stay away from the church until then as well, since Izek clearly didn't know she was lodging there and chose to visit—and ransack—the Blue Water Inn because he knew that's where the four party members were staying. The party left the church with a piece of parchment bearing Ireena's signature, which read "I, Ireena Kolyana, do hereby endorse the beheading of Izek Strazni"
