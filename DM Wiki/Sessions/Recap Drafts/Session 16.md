# Delivery for the Baron
- The session began with our Haunted Heroes' trek home from Lake Zarovich, where they'd just finished killing Izek Strazni. They were in high spirits; Choppy swung the burlap sack holding Izek's frozen head like a child with a candy-filled pillowcase on Halloween, while Arden used Izek's severed arm to make obscene and silly gestures at his friends. He's typically a dour and serious elf, but tonight he was giddy—drunk on the first taste of bona fide poetic justice he'd gotten to deliver since arriving in Barovia.
- Their first stop was Wachterhaus; it was about 8pm when they arrived. Arden used Izek's arm to knock on the front door. Haliq answered moments later, correctly assuming that the party was here to see the Lady and fetching her right away (but not before Arden used the severed arm to give the valet a cheerful wave in greeting). Lady Wachter accepted the frozen head from Choppy, expressing relief that the head was cold and therefore not yet putrid. She explained that she would now take some of her associates (Andrej, Boris, and Ruxandra) and confront the Baron at his residence (as described in [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#^Abstract-The-Transfer-of-Power]]), that the players were welcome to observe from afar but must stay out of sight, and that she would need a day to requisition the players' promised reward from the city's mismanaged and bloated armory once she officially took office. 
- Lady Wachter also discouraged the players from further violence against the Baron, saying that would likely foment more unrest than gratification amongst the townsfolk and that what her people needed now was clear-headed confidence and stability. 
- My prep notes for the scene between her and the Baron went like this:

> - Lady Wachter can't deliver the silvered goods immediately, as they must be requisitioned from one of the city's armories.
> - As a nod to the Tarokka reading, work in the phrase "ancient foe of a noble house" — perhaps "some will think me nothing more than the ancient foe of an old and noble house, but I assure you... whatever nobility once resided in House Vallakovich has long since been lost."
> - She'll invite them to a public transition of power the following morning in the town square.
> - Then she'll grab three of her cloaked associates (Boris, Andrej, Ruxandra) and head to the Baron's mansion. 
> - Vargas can greet her haughtily, still in his armor, though he won't suspect anything's immediately amiss: "To what, pray tell, do I owe the unexpected pleasure of your company?" 
> - Fiona: "Spare me the facade, Vargas. I'm here to facilitate your abdication."
> - Vargas, face hardening: "Now what a preposterous and seditious notion... All has been well and, once I've re-educated you within the walls of this noble house, all will continue to be well! IZEK! Arrest this muckraking malcontent!"
> - Fiona will toss Izek's head at Vargas' feet, and he'll tuck tail and run almost immediately. Ruxandra's *command* spell stops him in his tracks. Fiona then explains coolly and calmly that the Baron will abdicate his office in a public ceremony tomorrow morning, after which he and his family would keep their title and estate but be under indefinite house arrest, not to participate at all in administering the duties of Burgomaster. "Blink twice if you agree," Fiona will say.

- Having witnessed this efficient transfer of power from afar, the party retired to the Blue Water Inn to share the news of Izek's death with Urwin, Danika, Szoldar, Yevgeni, Rictavio, and the other gathered patrons. With input from Danika, Arden commissioned Yevgeni to craft a taxidermy trophy out of Izek's severed arm, one with articulated joints that could be manipulated to display any posture/gesture the owner desired. Arden would then give the stuffed arm as a gift to Urwin and Danika, for them to display among the other stuffed trophies on the walls of their taproom.
# The Abdication Ceremony
- The following morning, the abdication ceremony went exactly as described in [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F5a. The Abdication]]. Father Lucian observed everything from the party's side, commenting on the "mercy of the mists," sharing what little he knew about Ezra (chiefly that no pre-Strahd historical or liturgical text contains any mention of the goddess Ezra). He also expressed mild concern for his younger sister, Baroness Lydia, but confessed that she was unlikely even to notice much about the shift in her family's status because she already spent most of her time inside anyway.

# The Hunters' Shack
- Party then hoofed it to Szoldar and Yevgeni's hunting shack. They briefly paused on the way to confirm that Izek's body was still where they'd left it; it was, and had already begun to decompose. 
- Arriving at the hunters' shack, they found it as described in [[Arc E - The Missing Vistana#E4. Lake Zarovich]]. They noticed the standing stones, but left them alone at first while they knocked on the shack's open door and enlisted the help of the two hunters in traveling to Khazan's Tower. Szoldar and Yevgeni offered to serve as their guides for half their usual rate as a gesture of gratitude to the town's heroes, allowing the party to easily take both hunters with them. 
- Szoldar warned the party about the recent increase in werewolf aggression, surmising it must indicate a change in leadership in the pack which typically kept to the woods around Lake Baratok.
- Leaving the shack, the party asked the hunters about the standing stones; they answered by saying that they mostly left them alone, not knowing much about their origin or purpose but not wanting to incur the ire of whatever entities inspired their initial construction. The party remembered seeing similar standing stones near the Western Gate of Barovia (in [[Session 08 - Hairy Bill, Bairy Hill, and a Coin for the Ladies Three#The Svalich Road and the Raven Shrine]]), and left an offering of gold at this shrine as well, which they noticed bore an inscribed carving of a butterfly.

# The Road to Khazan's Tower
- We ran the scene at Luna River Bridge ([[Arc E - The Missing Vistana#E5a. Luna River Bridge]]), with Fig spotting and retrieving the linen handkerchief bearing the initials "R.V.R." and the other three party members recalling that the manuscript they'd received from [[Arturi Radanavich]] was written by a "Rudolph Van Richten," whose initials match those on the piece of cloth in Fig's hand. (That manuscript is here: [[A Study of the Werewolf's Curse]])
- we then ran a modified redux of the scarecrow-and-twig-blight encounter from [[Arc E - The Missing Vistana#E5b. Luna River Crossroads]], since the party had already encountered the first wave of these foes in [[Session 13 - The Lost and The Found#CSI Luna River]]. This time there were 3 scarecrows, but the party handled them easily and only sustained minor injuries; Choppy's fire magic was super effective against the dry, wooden foes. Also different about this encounter: the twig blights chattered about the entity who'd sent them, saying "the Lady of the Swamp sends her regards" to Fig and Arden when they drew near. 
- The party asked their guides, Szoldar and Yevgeni, about the identity of the "Lady of the Swamp". The hunters indicated that this could only refer to Baba Lysaga, the witch who dwelt in the swamp that used to be the village of Berez. They didn't know much else about the witch, however, because they made a rule of giving that swamp a wide berth—both because of the witch and because of the phase spiders known to inhabit the area (see [[Arc I - The Walls of Krezk#^Abstract-The-Megaliths]].

# So there's this *other* chicken, right...
- Party finally arrived at Lake Baratok after several hours, beholding the lake, the causeway, Ezmerelda's caravan, and the mage's tower as described in [[Arc E - The Missing Vistana#E6. Lake Baratok]]
- The hunters stayed on the far side of the causeway, not wishing to approach the mage's tower any more closely. Meanwhile the party focused first on Ezmerelda's wagon, reading the signs on the back door and wisely refraining from forcing their way in. Instead they knocked on the door and received a response in the form of perturbed clucking from within. Luckily Varnish could cast "Speak With Animals," and had a conversation with the chicken through the closed door. 
- Varnish learned that the chicken's name is "Eggsmerelda", as well as everything about Ezmerelda detailed in [[Arc E - The Missing Vistana#^Abstract-Eggsmerelda]], although the chicken wasn't angry with the party because they had not broken into the wagon and was happy to describe her owner in as much limited detail as a pet chicken can provide. Varnish learned that Ezmerelda had been gone for several days after departing to look for something in the woods, and that Eggsmerelda had heard the sounds of wolves trying to get into the wagon for the past several nights. 
- Fig and Choppy, noticing the other set of wagon tracks next to Ezmerelda's, investigated further. These tracks were a couple days older than those belonging to Ezmerelda's wagon, and appeared to lead south into the woods and then east toward Vallaki. Following the tracks down the causeway to a forest clearing just a few dozen yards from the lakeshore, they noticed spatterings of yellow paint on the underbrush and took a moment to recall where they'd seen that particular shade... the "aha!" came to all of them at once as they recalled seeing the same bright yellow on the side and signage of Rictavio's wagon in the Arasek Stockyard in [[Session 11 - No Bones About It#Confronting Henrik]] ("Rictavio's Carnival of Wonders!"). This was where the session ended: with the revelation that Rictavio was undeniably involved in Arabelle's kidnapping (see [[The Missing Vistana]]). Although the details of motive, means, and opportunity still eluded them, they knew now that Rictavio's wagon had been present at Khazan's Tower, which bore the same sigil as the [[DM Wiki/Entities/Items/Signet Ring|Signet Ring]] found at the site of Yan's beheading/Arabelle's kidnapping (in [[Session 13 - The Lost and The Found#CSI Luna River]]).
