# Breakfast with Ernst
- Arden went for his early-morning walk, checking in on the church from afar to confirm Ireena's continued safety, then stationed himself at the front door of the Blue Water Inn to await the arrival of Lady Wachter's associate
- Ernst arrived shortly before the rest of the party had awoken, so they came downstairs and entered the taproom to find Ernst and Arden already seated, Ernst tucking into his breakfast.
- Ernst mentioned wanting some coffee, prompting Arden to wonder aloud once again whether the coffee he carried was still edible. See, there'd been some confusion in [[Session 07 - The Tarokka Reading#Leaving Camp]] when Arden might have mixed up the pouch containing coffee grounds with the pouch containing the ashes of his murdered family. He first sought help from Urwin, who hadn't had coffee before and was unable to help. Then Arden posed the question to Ernst, who first questioned the provenance of Arden's coffee and learned that the paladin had been carrying around this same pouch of ground coffee for several decades, so it was no wonder the distinction between the coffee and the ashes was so difficult to make. The grounds were stale long before Arden came to Barovia, and their passage through the mists had likely only imparted some minor alterations to the coffee's profile to result in the ashen taste encountered by Arden and Stanimir back at the Tser Pool encampment. Ernst himself was unable to determine which pouch was which, ultimately concluding that neither could be safely considered coffee at this point and that neither should be used to brew any sort of beverage. 
- Coffee debate aside, Ernst delivered the poisoned wine and burlap sack along with a shred of parchment containing notes about Izek's daily schedule. %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F3. Return to the Blue Water Inn]] and [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F4. Izek’s Assassination]] %%
- Ernst assisted the players in formulating a plan for the day that was likely to find success: they would try to plant the tainted wine while Tereska was preparing Izek's lunch, and therefore should aim to be at the Vallakovich Manor shortly before noon. They would then keep tabs on Izek throughout the afternoon and tail him to the shores of Lake Zarovich, where they would ambush him.

# Visiting Parriwimple
- The party had the morning to attend to other matters, beginning with checking in on their pal Parriwimple, who'd been making himself useful among the refugees outside the town's Morning Gate. 
- The players noticed with concern that Parriwimple's werewolf bite hadn't appeared to heal at all in the four days since he'd sustained the injury, and they decided to tell him that he was, in all likelihood, cursed with lycanthropy. They assured him that they wouldn't let anything bad befall him and that they were searching for a cure or, at least, a treatment to lessen the curse's effects. They beseeched him to resist the curse's violent urges if they arised, and Parriwimple put on a brave face and promised that he wasn't scared, that he trusted the players, and that he would do his best to fight the curse. 
- They asked what Parriwimple had been up to these past several days, and he'd mostly been helping with manual labor—fetching and chopping firewood, digging postholes and latrines, hauling supplies when they arrived, fighting off wolves at night, and playing with the camp's children in his free time. 
- In a tense but sweet interaction, Arden said he'd missed Parriwimple and was glad he was doing well. Parriwimple's response at first put Arden off: "I haven't really missed you guys..." but he was quick to notice the dismay on his friend's face and clarified, "it's not that I've been thinking about you and was glad you weren't here... I've just been so happy being busy helping people and thinking about the people I'm helping that I haven't had time to miss you guys." %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc C - Into the Valley#Parriwimple's Mini-Arc]] for the background on Parriwimple's character development here. %%
- It was from Parriwimple that the party first learned about the disappearance of two children from camp: Fyodor and Myrtle. The party left Parriwimple to ask Emeric and Magda about the children's disappearance and Morgantha's possible involvement in it.

# Visiting Victor
- The players left the refugee camp and stopped by the Arasek general store, where Varnish purchased some improved leather armor while Arden inquired with the town's blacksmith about the expense and process of getting his sword silvered (though ultimately he didn't purchase the smith's services, remembering Lady Wachter's promise of reward in the form of silvered weapons from the previous night's dinner conversation). %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#Compensation]] %%
- They then beelined to the Vallakovich Manor, first to update Victor on their progress toward saving Stella and second to plant the poisoned wine. Claudia, the maid, welcomed the party in on the grounds that they were here to help with "the haunting", and then she tasked them with delivering Victor's lunch to him in the workroom since they were already planning on paying him a visit.
- In the attic, they told Victor what had happened at dinner the previous evening, especially their successful recruitment of Lady Wachter's belief and assistance with confronting the hags. They also shared what they'd learned about the hags' activities from Franz.
- Victor was relieved, if a little intimidated, to have Lady Wachter's direct assistance. He keyed in on the description of Franz's nightmare, noting that the onyx stone held to Franz's forehead was almost certainly the hag's heartstone, but he couldn't tell what to make of Franz's mention of the "heart of the windmill". With Victor's help, the players realized that they could learn the hags' true names by finding their coven contract. He'd been poring over old records and had found a schematic blueprint of the windmill, noticing a hidden compartment in the millstone's side, and bet his bottom dollar that that's where the hags kept their contract. 
- He insisted it would be crucial to handle the situation delicately, since the children were likely being held captive somewhere in the mill.

# Planting the Poisoned Wine
- Victor then asked about the rest of their conversation with Lady Fiona, and the party told him about their plans to kill Izek later that same day. Victor realized that he could be of great help in planting the wine in several ways: 1) he could help the party craft a viable plan for distracting Tereska, and 2) he could cast [[Greater Invisibility]] on whomever was going to do the actual, physical wine-bottle-switcheroo.
- The plan formed: Varnish, with his [[Actor (XPHB)]] feat, would don the disguise of Amal—the recently resigned head butler—and would serve as the distraction by entering the kitchen and attempting to make amends with Tereska. Meanwhile Fig, cloaked in Victor's invisibility spell, would sneak into the kitchen and swap out the wine bottles.
- The plan went off almost without any hitches: Varnish-as-Amal proved a worthy distraction, and Tereska fully believed she was speaking with her former coworker. Varnish-as-Amal failed to persuade her to let him rejoin the house staff, however, and Tereska gruffly shoed him out of the kitchen through the backdoor. Meanwhile, however, Fig silently snuck into the kitchen and expertly swapped the wine bottles (with a critical success on their sleight-of-hand check), then stayed behind to confirm that Tereska didn't notice the difference. %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F4a. Planting the Poisoned Wine]] for details on how Tereska responded to the wine-swap. %%
# Trailing Izek
- The party left the Manor and followed Izek from afar for the rest of the afternoon to make sure he drank the poisoned wine. They saw him extorting one of his "informants" and Varnish had to calm an angry dog to avoid drawing Izek's attention. Then they were nearly detected when Izek commanded one of his lackeys to retrieve something from a crate behind which the party was hiding; they luckily succeeded in hiding themselves. %%CLAUDE: cf. # The Fight at Lake Zarovich
- Reaching the lake to find Izek sitting and staring into space, the party approached silently. In a move of brilliant arrogance, Arden crafted a paper airplane from the parchments given to him by Ireena (bearing her endorsement) and Ernst (bearing Izek's schedule) and threw it at Izek, who'd begun to feel the effects of the poisoned wine and was midway through a fumbling attempt to read the letters when Arden attacked him by surprise. Though Arden got the first strike, the party agreed that the final blow would be delivered by Choppy, who wanted to live up to his namesake by beheading the vile man. %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Arc F - Lady Wachter's Wish#F4c. Ambushing Izek]] for the fight's setting and general narrative course. %%
- The fight was heated in more ways than one. Arden's initial ambush was devastatingly effective, but the brute was able to regain his footing and use reactions to simultaneously retaliate and improve his position. All the while, the party hurled insults and curses at Izek, while he growled and roared in rage and fury. Choppy again indulged his pyromaniac streak by casting [[Fireball]], using metamagic to carefully sculpt the spell's effects so they didn't harm Fig, who stood next to Izek. He was dismayed, however, when Izek laughed and shrugged off the attack. 
- The party quickly reduced Izek's first form to 0 hp, thinking themselves victorious for a brief moment until the fiendish villain erupted into his fiery second form. With malice in his voice, he targeted Choppy and very nearly killed the sorcerer while leaving the other three party members to burn in a scorching line of searing flames. 
- At one point Arden landed a blow that Izek caught with his devilish hand, the blade digging into the flesh of his palm and becoming superheated, forcing Arden to drop the greatsword. A seasoned fighter, Arden was able to employ his dagger to continue his onslaught of righteous attacks. 
- When things were beginning to look truly dire—Choppy on death's door, Varnish running low on healing spells, Arden wrestling with his white-hot blade—Fig landed a truly heroic [[Stunning Strike]] that rendered Izek inert for the final moments of his life. With the brute unable to move, attack, or use any of his devastating reactions, it fell to Choppy to deliver Izek's demise. He first hurled a [[Chromatic Orb (XPHB)]] of ice at Izek's thick and bulging neck, freezing the man-turned-demon from the shoulders up. Then he produced two daggers from his jerkin and launched himself at Izek, wielding the blades like icepicks to chip away at the frozen column of Izek's neck—a successful beheading, although one which took a grotesquely long time and left Choppy covered in frozen bits of flesh which quickly melted amid the heat from the nearby fires. 
- With Izek dead, Arden picked up his silvered battleaxe and used it to cleave Izek's devilish arm from his body—he would take it to investigate the source of Izek's power, although he certainly didn't mind how it hung like a trophy when strapped across his broad chest and back. Arden also found an amber shard in Izek's possession, which he removed and handed to Varnish for safekeeping. Together the two of them were able to perceive that, while the arm seemed mostly inert now (and may indeed just be a now-dead piece of demonized flesh), the amber shard radiated a muted but palpable aura of abjurative magic. %%CLAUDE: cf. [[Amber Shards]] for details on this shard, which is the 'shard of Seriach' %%
- With the dark deed done, the party placed Izek's head in the burlap sack and set off southward, toward Vallaki, to report their success to the Lady Wachter.
